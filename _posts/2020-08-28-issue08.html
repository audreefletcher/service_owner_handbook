Digital transformation isn't a thing you buy, it's a thing you do

The final article in a five-part series talking about the internet-era CTO, untangling technology spaghetti, and how to make those build-versus-buy decisions. Takeaways: remember that integration requires code too; it's best to own what differentiates you; and the value of keeping your options open because nothing stays the same for very long.


Dominic Cummings meets Keanu Reeves

A thought-provoking blog post from Rachel Coldicutt - and a timely challenge on the need for better governance around (especially) newer government technology. If you haven't come across Rachel before, look her up. She was the CEO of @doteveryone and has written many an inspiring piece on ethics, regulation and digital technology.


Codesigning for distributed systems leadership

Over at MHCLG, Paul Maltby and his team are doing a cracking job at working with Local Authorities to design the future of the planning system together. In this short Twitter thread he outlines many of the things they've done and are doing - with links to a great deal of good work and good people. Paul's team does an amazing job of working in the open - extending to a fully public roadmap and even livestreamed show-and-tells. I think there's a lot we can learn here when it comes to thinking through our system leadership role with our sectors - after all, they're focused on a similar problem: how best to design and deliver nationally-scaleable, interoperable systems and services that meet local needs.
