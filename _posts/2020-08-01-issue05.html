Failure demand

A nice little blog post from Stephen Gill looking at failure demand as applied to public digital services. Highly recommended, because failure demand is something I don't think we give enough thought to as a department. If you want to go a bit deeper, here's a webinar from the most prominent thinker on failure demand, John Seddon.

Multisourcing

Essentially extending the logic of Simon Wardley - who looked at maturity of technologies to inform decisions about build/re-use/rent/buy - into sourcing strategy. Alex Holmes talks about moving away from binary choices and one-size-fits-all methods that rely heavily on systems integration. Pair this article with one from MOJ CDIO Tom Read who lays out their step-by-step process for working through sourcing decisions.

Alpha to live is not a linear progression

The linearity suggested in GDS diagrams showing Discovery>Alpha>Beta>Live is frustratingly misleading - indeed, as with most models, following it unquestioningly causes some serious problems and missed opportunities. Michael Brunton-Spall in this article lays out some of the thinking to avoid in this article - with particularly great challenges around the role of alpha, and the observation that, if you're truly prototyping lots of different ideas, you should think of them as separate alphas, expect a much higher failure rate, and plan to throw away your alpha code before progressing anything into beta. MBS then follows it up with a corker of an article here describing them instead by their intent and so dividing things into discovery, prototyping, MVP, build and live, spelling out how different each phase is from the next.
